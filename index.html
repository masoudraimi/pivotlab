The real barrier to data careers

Breaking into data is broken
for most career changers

Data careers are high-growth and increasingly in-demand. But the path is broken. Generic bootcamps assume coding experience, price out anyone with constraints, and leave you completely alone when it gets hard. People quit not because the content is too hard, but because there's no one to say "ignore that for now." And even the people who finish often can't get hired, because employers no longer trust a certificate or a CV to tell them who can actually do the work.

1 in 3

Bootcamp graduates remain unemployed in their target field six months after completion. Accelerated courses often fail to build deep expertise, leaving job-seekers stranded.

$10k–$17k+

The average upfront cost of a data bootcamp. Despite the investment, most programs skip critical fundamentals, leaving graduates underprepared for real enterprise work.

The proof gap

Even career changers who finish a course have nothing concrete to show. A certificate from a platform with 50,000 identical reviews means nothing to a hiring manager.

The confidence trap

Most people quit because hitting a wall feels like evidence they're not smart enough. They were never told that confusion is normal. The wall is the course, not them.

The six pillars

What employers actually
hire for

Technical foundations

SQL, Python and visualisation, taught thoroughly by industry practitioners. These are the table stakes for a data role, not the thing that separates strong hires from weak ones.

Commercial readiness

How real data teams actually operate. Scoping work, communicating findings, working with stakeholders. The ways of working that make a new hire useful from day one.

Professional judgment

Knowing what to ignore, what to escalate, and how to move forward when the answer isn't clear. The named hiring differentiator, built through real scenarios, not lectures.

Real project portfolio

Original work on real data, scoped like commercial projects. The kind of proof a hiring manager can review and interrogate. This is what beats a CV.

Mentorship and cohort

Cohorts of 15 to 25 peers pivoting alongside you. Near-peer mentors who've made the jump. When you hit a wall, someone who says "ignore that for now," not silence.

Employer vouching

Trust-by-proxy into hiring teams. We only introduce candidates we're confident about. That reference from us is often what gets you past the CV pile and into a real interview.
